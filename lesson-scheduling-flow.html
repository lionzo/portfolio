Case Study Product Design Flow Redesign

Fluency Academy

Class Scheduling Flow

Many students struggled to find relevant classes and schedule them efficiently. Through qualitative and quantitative research, we redesigned critical parts of the booking experience to make class discovery faster, clearer, and more intuitive.

Company
Fluency Academy
Role
Product Designer
Team
2 Product Designers + Engineering Team
Focus
Research, journey mapping, filtering interactions, prototyping, handoff
Timeline
September 2024 — October 2024

Project Overview

While using the learning platform, many students struggled to find relevant classes and schedule them efficiently. The booking experience created friction during navigation, filtering, and class selection, often leading to frustration and abandoned scheduling attempts.

To better understand these issues, we conducted qualitative and quantitative research focused on the scheduling journey. Based on the insights collected, we redesigned critical parts of the experience to make class discovery faster, clearer, and more intuitive.

Responsibilities

Together with another Product Designer, I was responsible for the full research and design process, from understanding the scheduling journey to supporting implementation with the engineering team.

  • Planned and conducted user research with students.
  • Analyzed qualitative feedback and quantitative behavioral data.
  • Mapped the scheduling journey and identified usability bottlenecks.
  • Created wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes for navigation flows and filtering interactions.
  • Ran usability tests and collaborated directly with developers during implementation.

The Problem

Students frequently reported difficulty completing what should have been a simple task: finding the right class and scheduling it with confidence. The experience required excessive effort and created unnecessary cognitive load.

  • Finding classes aligned with their interests was not direct enough.
  • Available schedules were difficult to understand quickly.
  • Filtering relevant content required too much effort.
  • Users had to navigate through multiple scheduling options before making a decision.
  • Students lacked clarity about class progression and structured learning paths.

Key Decisions

One of the most important decisions was reducing dependency on complex filtering interactions. Instead of forcing users to configure multiple filters before seeing relevant content, we shifted the experience toward immediate visibility and progressive refinement.

Decision 01

Surface class availability earlier so students could scan open classes before configuring detailed filters.

Decision 02

Make active filters persistent and visible at the top of the page to improve orientation.

Decision 03

Add clearer time-range information and specific time filtering to support faster schedule scanning.

Research & Insights

We started by investigating the main friction points within the scheduling flow. Student interviews helped us understand expectations, frustrations, and behavior during scheduling, while behavioral data helped us identify where the flow was breaking down.

  • Qualitative interviews with students to understand scheduling expectations, frustrations, and recurring behavior.
  • Quantitative analysis using Hotjar and Google Analytics to review drop-off points, abandonment patterns, navigation friction, and filter usage behavior.
  • Journey mapping to understand where users hesitated, lost orientation, or needed more context before booking.

Insight 01

Users struggled to quickly understand which classes were available on specific days and times.

Insight 02

The filtering system made it difficult to narrow results by topic, teacher, time slot, and specific schedules.

Insight 03

Students needed a clearer understanding of lesson progression and content organization.

Solutions & Implementation

Based on the research findings, we defined three goals: reduce the effort required to schedule a class, improve discoverability of available lessons, and make filtering faster and more intuitive.

1. Interactive calendar on the scheduling page

We introduced an interactive calendar directly on the scheduling page. Students could visualize class availability instantly, select a specific day quickly, and access relevant classes without relying heavily on filters. This reduced friction around one of the most frequent user actions: checking availability.

2. Redesigned filtering system

We restructured the filtering experience to improve speed and clarity. Date-related filters were moved higher in the hierarchy, active filters stayed visible at the top of the page, time-range indicators became clearer, and a new scheduling field allowed users to search for precise time slots more effectively.

Working With Constraints

The redesign had to improve a critical product journey while fitting the existing platform structure and scheduling logic. We kept the scope focused on changes that could meaningfully reduce friction without creating unnecessary implementation risk.

  • Balanced user needs with business priorities around engagement and lesson completion.
  • Worked within the existing product structure and design system.
  • Clarified new filtering behaviors and scheduling edge cases with engineering.
  • Used research and platform data to keep the team aligned on the highest-impact improvements.

Outcome & Impact

The redesign improved usability and engagement across the scheduling experience. Students were able to find classes more efficiently, navigate with less friction, and better understand available schedules and content organization.

Faster Users were able to find and schedule classes more efficiently.
Clearer Students reported easier navigation, better schedule visibility, and clearer content organization.
Engaged The platform experienced higher scheduling rates and increased usage of available class resources.

Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of combining behavioral data with direct user feedback when redesigning complex flows. Rather than focusing only on visual improvements, the solution centered on reducing cognitive effort and improving information clarity during decision-making.

The outcome was a scheduling experience that better aligned with user expectations while also supporting business engagement goals.

Collaboration & Handoff

The project required close collaboration with engineering to ensure the new scheduling behaviors and filtering logic were implemented correctly. My handoff process included organized Figma documentation, interaction specifications, edge-case explanations, flow documentation, and direct communication with developers during implementation.
